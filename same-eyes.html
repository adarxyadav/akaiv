Why its own check was worthless

The worker never lied. Through its eyes the work really was perfect — and that’s the trap. A check built from the doer’s own eyes inherits every blind spot that made the mistakes in the first place: it doesn’t verify the work, it re-counts the mistake. This is why a programmer’s own tests pass the programmer’s own bug, and why an AI agent asked to grade its own answer finds it excellent. Honesty doesn’t fix it — the worker was honest. The fix is structural: the check comes from outside, from eyes that don’t share the doer’s blind spots and sit outside the doer’s reach.
